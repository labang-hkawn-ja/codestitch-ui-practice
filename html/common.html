About the Common Dolphin

The common dolphin (Delphinus delphis) is known for its agility and distinct color pattern with a yellowish hourglass on its side. They are social animals often seen in large pods.

Key Characteristics

  • Appearance: Sleek body with a distinctive hourglass pattern of yellow and grey.
  • Size: Typically 1.7 to 2.4 meters in length.
  • Lifespan: Around 25-35 years in the wild.
  • Habitat: Found in warm-temperate and tropical oceans worldwide.

More About Common Dolphins

Common dolphins are fast swimmers and acrobatic, often leaping from the water. They communicate with a range of whistles and clicks and form complex social bonds within their pods.

Interesting Facts

  • Speedy Swimmers: Can reach speeds up to 60 km/h (37 mph).
  • Social: Pods can include hundreds of individuals.
  • Diet: Feed primarily on small fish and squid.
  • Conservation: Considered Least Concern but threatened by fishing nets and pollution.
